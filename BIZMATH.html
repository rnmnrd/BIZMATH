Compound Interest

Definition of Terms

Term Definition
Compound Interest (IC) An interest from the periodic addition of simple interest to the principal amount or simply the difference between the compound amount and the principal
Future Value(F) An accumulated amount obtained by adding the principal and the compound interest
Frequency of Conversion (m) The number of conversion periods in one year.
Common conversion periods are:
  • Annually   (m = 1)
  • Semi-annually   (m = 2)
  • Quarterly   (m = 4)
  • Monthly   (m = 12)
  • Daily   (m = 365)
Conversion or Interest Period (t) The time between successive conversion of interest.
Total number of conversion periods (n) The total number of conversion periods within the time period.
$$n=mt=\left ( \text{frequency of conversion} \right )\left ( \text{time} \right )$$
Nominal Rate (r) The annual rate interest
Rate (i) The interest rate for each conversion period.
$$i=\frac{r}{m}=\frac{\text{annual rate interest}}{\text{frequency of conversion}}$$
Present Value (P) The value that will accumulate to the future value if there is an interest at periodic rate for conversion period

Simple Annuity

Definition of Terms

Term Definition
Simple Annuity An annuity where the payment interval is the same as the interest period
Payment Interval Time between successive payments
Term of an Annuity (t) Time between the first payment interval and the last payment interval
Regular or Periodic Payment (R) The amount of each payment
Amount (Future Value) of an Annuity (F) Sum of the future values of all the payments to be made during the entire term of the annuity
Present Value of an Annuity (P) Sum of the present values of all the payments to be made during the entire term of the annuity

Bonds

Concepts about Bonds

Definition of Terms

Bond: interest-bearing security which promises to pay:

  1. a stated amount of money on the maturity date, and
  2. regular interest payments called coupons.

Coupon: periodic interest payment that the bondholder receives during the time between purchase date and maturity date; usually received semi-annually.

Coupon Rate: the rate per coupon payment period, denoted by "r"

Price of a Bond: the price of the bond at purchase time; denoted by "P"

Par Value or Face Value the amount payable or the maturity date; denoted by "F"

  1. If P = F, the bond is purchased at par.
  2. If P < F, the bond is purchased at a discount.
  3. If P > F, the bond is purchased at a premium.

Term of a Bond: fixed period of time (in years) at which the bond is redeemable as stated in the bond certificate; number of years from time of purchase to maturity date

Fair Price Bond: present value of all cash inflows to the bondholder

Lesson 1: Business Loans and Consumer Loans

Objectives

  • Illustrate business and consumer loans.
  • Distinguish between business and consumer loans.

Definition of Terms

Business Loan: Money borrowed specifically for business purposes (e.g., start-up or expansion).

Consumer Loan: Money borrowed by an individual for personal or family needs.

Collateral: Assets (e.g., house, investments) used to secure a loan.

Term of the Loan: The time allowed to pay back the entire loan.

Basics of Consumer Loans vs. Business Loans

Aspect Consumer Loan Business Loan
Purpose Personal or family needs Business start-up or expansion
Collateral May be required May be required
Guarantor Not usually required Usually required
Requirements Credit report, income tax returns, payslips Credit report, bank statements, business financials
Loan Term Generally longer Generally shorter
Interest Rate Lower Higher

Objective

Definition of Terms

Amortization: Paying a loan in equal installments.

Mortgage: Loan secured by collateral (usually real estate).

Chattel Mortgage: Loan on movable property.

Outstanding Balance: Remaining debt at a given time.

Compound Interest

Definition of Terms

Term Definition
Compound Interest (IC) An interest from the periodic addition of simple interest to the principal amount or simply the difference between the compound amount and the principal
Future Value(F) An accumulated amount obtained by adding the principal and the compound interest
Frequency of Conversion (m) The number of conversion periods in one year.
Common conversion periods are:
  • Annually   (m = 1)
  • Semi-annually   (m = 2)
  • Quarterly   (m = 4)
  • Monthly   (m = 12)
  • Daily   (m = 365)
Conversion or Interest Period (t) The time between successive conversion of interest.
Total number of conversion periods (n) The total number of conversion periods within the time period.
$$n=mt=\left ( \text{frequency of conversion} \right )\left ( \text{time} \right )$$
Nominal Rate (r) The annual rate interest
Rate (i) The interest rate for each conversion period.
$$i=\frac{r}{m}=\frac{\text{annual rate interest}}{\text{frequency of conversion}}$$
Present Value (P) The value that will accumulate to the future value if there is an interest at periodic rate for conversion period

Bonds

Definition of Terms

Term Definition
Bonds A form of debt financing or raising money by borrowing from investors.
Bond An interest-bearing security which promises to pay
    a. stated amount of money on the maturity date
    b. regular interest payments
Coupon Periodic interest payment that the bondholder receives during the time between purchase date and maturity date, usually received semi-annually.
Coupon Rate The rate per coupon payment period; denoted by r
Price of a Bond The price of the bond at the purchcase time denoted by P.
Par Value or Face Value The amount payable or the maturity date; dentoed by F.
  • If P = F, the bond is purchased at par.
  • If P < F, the bond is purchased at a discount.
  • If P > F, the bond is purchased at a premium.
Term of a Bond Fixed period of time (in years) at which the bond is redeemable as stated in the bond certificate; number of years from time of purchase to maturity date.
Fair Price Bond Present value of all cash inflows to the bondholder.
